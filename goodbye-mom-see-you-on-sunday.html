16. beda goodbye. see you when I see you dan so long


These expressions of farewell or parting, 'goodbye', 'see you when I see you 'and 'so long' have slightly different in their usage. Two of them, 'goodbye' and 'so long' have similar meaning, while the other one 'see you when I see you' has rather different meaning.  

Pembahasan:

These three expressions are used in slightly different contexts. The explanation is as follows:

'Goodbye' and 'so long' are used when you do not expect to see someone you're parting anymore in the near future. 'Goodbye' can be used in formal or informal conversation, while 'so long' is more suitable in informal situation. 'see you when I see you' is more suitable for informal situation and rather intimate conversation like between best friends or lovers. This expression is normally uttered when you expect to see the person you're parting in the near future.
