16. What do you learn after reading Trifles?​


Jawaban:

The main themes in Trifles are gender, isolation, and justice. Gender: the male characters only want to gather evidence of Minnie's crime, whereas the women come to understand the emotional pain that drove Minnie to murder her husband.

Trifiles Symbolize: "Trifles, is a symbol reflecting how men view women. A "trifle" is something that is small, of little value or importance, or of no consequence. Throughout the play, Glaspell uses dialogue which allows us to see the demeaning view the men have of the women.

The play "Trifles" takes place in the dirty, chaotic kitchen of Mrs. Minnie Wright on one cold winter day. The sheriff, the attorney, and a neighbor come to the house to investigate the death of Mr. ...  As the group inspects the house the tiny details (or trifles) paint the picture of what has occurred in the house. The conflict that seems to be most evident in the play Trifles is the abrogation of the basic rights for safety and liberty, that all females deserve, by a male-dominated society. The turning point, or climax, of a play, occurs at the high dramatic point of the story when the conflict begins to be resolved. In Susan Glaspell's play "Trifles," the climax occurs when Mrs. Peters and Mrs. Hale decide to hide a dead bird from authorities investigating a murder case.
